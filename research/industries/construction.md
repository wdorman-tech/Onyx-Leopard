## Industry-Specific Metrics

**Bonding Capacity (Critical Constraint)**
The primary growth limiter for small-to-mid GCs. Sureties set aggregate capacity at 10–20× adjusted working capital (current assets minus current liabilities, with receivables >90 days removed). Net worth of $1M typically supports aggregate program of $10M. Single project limit is typically 1.5–2× the contractor's largest successfully completed project. SBA Surety Guarantee Program covers up to $9M ($14M on federal contracts) for contractors without an established surety history.

**Backlog (Forward Revenue Visibility)**
Total signed-contract value not yet recognized as revenue. Expressed as months of work: Backlog ÷ (Annual Revenue ÷ 12). Industry target: 6–9 months of backlog. Below 3 months signals business development crisis. Above 18 months signals capacity constraint or overbidding. Backlog-to-working-capital ratio should stay below 5:1 to avoid cash flow stress.

**Bid-Hit Ratio (Estimating Efficiency)**
Number of bids won ÷ total bids submitted. Competitive/public bid work benchmark: 25–40% win rate. Negotiated/relationship work: 50–70% win rate. Tracking cost-per-bid-dollar-won is the real efficiency metric — if it costs $15K to produce a bid and you win 1 in 4 at $500K contract value, your effective BD cost is 1.2% of revenue.

**Profit Fade (Gross Profit Fade)**
The delta between estimated gross margin at bid and actual gross margin at project close. Industry problem: projects frequently start at 14–18% estimated GM and close at 8–12% actual GM. Causes: scope gaps in estimates, unrecovered change orders, labor overruns, subcontractor defaults. Best practice: monthly WIP review comparing estimated-cost-at-completion to original budget. CFMA best-in-class contractors show gross margin of 21.8% vs. industry average of ~14.8%.

**Experience Modification Rate (EMR)**
Workers' compensation experience modifier. Industry baseline: 1.0. Excellent: below 0.75. Problematic: above 1.2. Impact: EMR of 1.2 means 20% workers' comp premium surcharge and exclusion from many owner prequalification lists. EMR below 0.85 correlates with 25% more contract wins (LumberFi). Many public and federal project owners use 1.0 as a hard prequalification cutoff. Formula: (Actual losses ÷ Expected losses) adjusted over 3-year rolling period.

**WIP Schedule (Work in Progress)**
The core financial document for construction companies. Required monthly by most sureties and banks. Contains for every active project: contract value, total costs incurred, estimated cost to complete, percent complete, billings to date, and revenue recognized. Overbilling (billing ahead of cost incurred) creates a short-term liability; underbilling is a short-term asset. Consistent overbilling improves cash float but red-flags sureties. CFMA: Days in accounts receivable benchmark 56.6 days (2024); target below 60 days.

**Change Order Rate**
Change orders as a percentage of original contract value. Well-managed GC with thorough estimating: 3–8% change order rate. Poorly scoped projects or owner-driven changes: 15–30%+. Change order approval speed is a cash flow variable — unresolved T&M change orders are the most common cause of underbilling blowouts. Tracking change order approval lag (days from submission to approval) is a leading indicator of project financial health.

**Net Profit Margin**
CFMA 2024 Benchmarker (2023 fiscal year): industry average 6.3% net income before tax. CFMA 2025 Benchmarker (2024 fiscal year): rose to 6.7%. Best-in-class performers: 11.9%. By segment — specialty trades: 6.9%; heavy highway: 7.2%; industrial/nonresidential: 4.1%. SG&A expenses: industry average 11.8% of revenue; best-in-class 10.8%. G&A target: 8–15% of revenue.

**Revenue Per Employee**
CFMA 2024: $450,086 revenue per FTE (up from $410,509 in 2022). CFMA 2025: record high of $514,587. Micro firms (1–4 employees): $145K–$148K/employee. Enterprise (500+ employees): $412K–$420K/employee. Scaling revenue per employee is the clearest sign of operational leverage — it reflects better project selection, tighter estimating, and effective subcontractor management.

**Overhead Rate**
G&A overhead as % of revenue: small GCs (<$50M): 20–25%; mid-size ($50M–$100M): 15–20%; large (>$100M): 10–15%. Urban markets add 3–6% to overhead vs. rural. CFMA best-in-class and average SG&A rates are similar — profitability differences between best-in-class and average companies come primarily from superior direct cost control, not lower overhead.

**Current Ratio / Working Capital**
Current assets ÷ current liabilities. CFMA benchmark: 1.6:1. Quick ratio: 1.4:1. This is the #1 metric sureties use to set bonding limits. Below 1.2 triggers surety program re-evaluation. Days in cash benchmark: 27–90 days of operating expenses in liquid form.

**Return on Equity (ROE)**
Net income ÷ total equity. CFMA 2024: industry average 31.4%; best-in-class 59.7%. Target: 15–20% minimum. Construction ROE runs high relative to assets because most contractors operate with thin equity and high revenue leverage.

**Days in Accounts Receivable**
(Accounts Receivable ÷ Annualized Revenue) × 365. CFMA 2024: industry average 56.6 days. Target: 40–60 days. Above 75 days indicates collection problems or retainage accumulation — both cash flow killers.

| 4 — Growing GC | $5M–$20M | 15–50 | $5M–$15M single; $15M–$50M aggregate | Senior PM, superintendent hire, safety manager (contracted), preconstruction capabilities begin, first GMP pursuit, estimating department forms, regional job site trailers |
