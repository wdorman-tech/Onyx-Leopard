# Retail (Brick-and-Mortar Chain) — Node Taxonomy

> Research basis: NRF National Retail Security Survey 2023; NRF 2025 Annual Retail Sales Forecast; ICSC Marketplaces Industry Report Q4-2023; Census Bureau Advance Monthly Retail Sales (MARTS); Placer.ai 2024 Foot Traffic Recap; Shopify retail cost benchmarks 2025; Retalon GMROI benchmarks; Retail Owners Institute; Dollar General / Five Below growth histories (Wikipedia, Motley Fool, Fortune); Costco / REI / Trader Joe's structural analysis; Extensiv 3PL Pricing Guide 2025; International Franchise Association 2024; NielsenIQ slotting fee data; CFPB co-branded card report 2023; Deloitte co-branded credit card partnerships analysis.

## Locations

|---|---|---|---|

| Regional Mall Inline Tenant | 5+ stores OR $1M/yr revenue; credit requirements | $8,000–$35,000+ (B-mall $20–60/sq ft/yr; A-mall $50–200/sq ft/yr) | High foot traffic; brand elevation; CAM + percentage-of-sales clause common |

| E-Commerce Fulfillment Center | E-commerce launched; $500K+ online revenue | $10,000–$40,000 (or 3PL: $833–$16,667/mo based on $10K–$200K/yr 3PL range) | Online order fulfillment; ship-from-store alternative; enables BOPIS (buy online pick up in store) |
| Dark Store (Ship-from-Store Hub) | 30+ stores; omnichannel strategy active | $3,000–$10,000 (converted or dedicated store-format DC) | Last-mile delivery cost -20%; inventory held closer to customer; reduces central DC pressure |
| Training Store / Lab Store | 20+ stores; HR department active | $2,000–$8,000 (dedicated training space or retrofitted store) | New hire ramp time -30%; consistent brand standards; tests new merchandising concepts before chain-wide rollout |
| International Store — Canada / Mexico | 100+ domestic stores; $100M+ domestic revenue | $8,000–$30,000 (higher regulatory/compliance overhead) | International revenue stream; brand global reach; unlocks duty drawback and cross-border logistics |

## Industry-Specific Metrics

**Same-Store Sales (Comps / Comparable Store Sales):** Revenue growth in stores open at least 12 months, excluding new openings. Benchmark: +3.0% signals healthy consumer spending; Walmart posted +4.6% and Costco +6.4% in late 2024. Negative comps for two consecutive quarters trigger review of store-level pricing, assortment, and marketing strategy.

**Sales per Square Foot (SPF):** Annual revenue divided by total selling area. National average across retail types: $325/sq ft. Specialty retail benchmark: $300–$500/sq ft. Grocery: $300–$600/sq ft. Luxury outliers: Apple ~$5,500/sq ft, Tiffany ~$3,000/sq ft. Below $150/sq ft in a non-discounter context signals underperformance. Used in lease negotiations, store format decisions, and capital allocation.

**Inventory Turnover:** COGS divided by average inventory value. Retail-wide average: 10.86–11.32 turns/yr (Retalon 2024). Category benchmarks: grocery 14+ turns, fashion 4–6 turns, furniture 4–6 turns. Below 3 turns signals overbuying or trend miss; above 20 risks stockouts. Dollar General targets 5–8 turns on its limited SKU model.

**GMROI (Gross Margin Return on Investment):** Gross profit divided by average inventory cost. Measures how efficiently inventory investment generates gross profit. Benchmark: above $2.00 is strong (family clothing avg $2.0; electronics avg $1.86; hardware $1.77; lumber/building $3.05 per Retail Owners Institute). Below $1.00 means the inventory is destroying margin.

**Shrinkage Rate:** Inventory losses from shoplifting, employee theft, vendor fraud, and administrative error. Industry average 1.6% of sales (up from 1.4% in FY2021 per NRF). U.S. retailers lost ~$45B to shoplifting in 2024 alone; global shrink projected at $132B. Self-checkout shrink rate 3.5% vs. staffed checkout 0.2%. Every 0.1% reduction in shrink on a $10M revenue store recovers $10,000/year.

**Conversion Rate:** Percentage of store visitors who make a purchase. National average: 20–27%; range 16–40% depending on format. Mall inline stores see lower rates (~15–20%) due to browse-only traffic. Discount stores and convenience formats see higher rates (35–45%). A 1% improvement in conversion at 1,000 daily visitors with $45 ATV = $450/day incremental revenue.

**Average Transaction Value (ATV) / Basket Size:** Revenue divided by number of transactions. Benchmarks by format: convenience store avg $12.12 in-store; grocery $40–$60; general specialty retail $20–$80; luxury/jewelry $436+ online. A $1.00 increase in ATV across 500 daily transactions = $15,000/month in incremental revenue.

**Foot Traffic:** Total visitor count per day/week/month measured by door counters or mobile data (Placer.ai). Drives denominator for conversion rate. Mall traffic is down structurally from peak 2000s levels but stabilized in 2023–2024 for high-quality centers. Retailers use Placer.ai or similar tools at $500–$3,000/mo to benchmark against competitors.

**Sell-Through Rate:** Percentage of received inventory sold within a defined period (typically 4–8 weeks for fashion). Target: 40–60% in first month for fashion. Below 40% triggers early markdown; approaching 100% risks stockouts. Tracked per SKU, style, or vendor for buying decisions.

**Shrinkage by Source (NRF breakdown):** External theft (shoplifting + ORC) ~37%, employee theft ~29%, process/administrative error ~20%, vendor fraud ~6%, other ~8%.

**Labor Cost as % of Revenue:** Healthy benchmark is 12–15% for discount/value retail, 18–25% for specialty. At 60% annual turnover (BLS retail average), a 100-person store incurs ~$600K/yr in turnover cost at $10K/event.

**Occupancy Cost as % of Revenue:** Rent + CAM + utilities + insurance. Target: 8–12% of store revenue. Above 15% signals the location is underperforming for its rent burden. Used to assess lease renewal decisions and underperforming store closure triggers.

## Growth Stages

| Stage | Threshold | What Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Founder-Operator (Single Store) | 1 store; $0–$500K annual revenue; 1–5 employees | Pop-up or first permanent location; owner-operator handles all functions; POS vendor; basic insurance; local bank account |
| Proven Unit (Profitable First Store) | 1 store; $500K–$1.5M annual revenue; positive 4-wall EBITDA | Second location feasibility; first non-owner store manager hire; wholesale vendor credit terms improve; local marketing investment |
| Local Chain (2–5 Stores) | 2–5 stores; $1M–$5M annual revenue; 20–75 employees | District-level thinking begins; inventory management software justified; commercial real estate broker relationship; basic HR function; loss prevention awareness |
| Regional Player (6–25 Stores) | 6–25 stores; $5M–$25M annual revenue; 75–400 employees | First regional distribution center; district manager(s); buying and merchandising department; dedicated HR; loyalty program launch; ERP evaluation; potential investor interest |
| Regional Chain (26–75 Stores) | 26–75 stores; $25M–$100M annual revenue; 400–1,500 employees | Regional VP layer; planogram department; visual merchandising team; private label exploration; e-commerce department; co-branded card partnership exploration; vendor slotting program; real estate/site selection team |
| Emerging National (76–200 Stores) | 76–200 stores; $100M–$500M annual revenue; 1,500–5,000 employees | Second regional DC or national DC; franchise program evaluation; retail media network; full CMO function; LP department with field investigators; dedicated supply chain and demand planning teams; potential IPO or PE investment |
| National Chain (201–1,000 Stores) | 201–1,000 stores; $500M–$3B annual revenue; 5,000–30,000+ employees | Multiple DCs (Dollar General model: open ~1,000/yr at this stage); international exploration; full retail media advertising platform; dedicated sustainability/ESG function; acquisition of smaller regional competitors; CFO and investor relations function; warehouse club format consideration |
| National Powerhouse / Destination Retailer (1,000+ Stores) | 1,000+ stores; $3B+ annual revenue | National distribution network fully optimized; international expansion (Canada, Mexico, EU); full vertically-integrated private label; co-branded credit card at scale; IPO or public market operations; M&A war chest; dedicated government affairs; sophisticated retail media network rivaling CPG ad budgets |
