## Industry-Specific Metrics

**Enrollment (Active Students):** Total paying or enrolled students at a given time. K-12 private schools average 166 students/school (NCES); bootcamps average 20–60 students/cohort; online platforms measure by monthly active learners. Trigger for most node unlocks.

**Student Acquisition Cost (SAC / CAC):** All-in cost to enroll one paying student. Benchmarks: B2C online course $50–$300; bootcamp $2,000–$4,000; B2B K-12 district $1,143 avg (FinancialModelsLab); university (OPM channel) $3,000–$8,000. Target: SAC < 20% of LTV.

**Lifetime Value (LTV) Per Student:** Total gross revenue attributable to one student. Bootcamp: $13,728 one-time LTV; subscription EdTech: ARPU $115/yr × avg tenure; K-12 school: $15,000/yr × avg 3.2 yrs = $48,000; corporate training seat: $200–$2,000/yr × contract years. LTV:CAC ratio target 3:1 minimum.

**Completion Rate (Course / Cohort):** Percent of enrolled students who finish the program. MOOC/self-paced median: 5–15% (Coursera avg 6%); bootcamp target: 80–90%; corporate training target: 70–85%; K-12 semester course: 90%+. Low completion rate is a leading indicator of churn and NPS collapse.

**Placement Rate (Career Programs):** Percent of bootcamp/vocational graduates employed in field within 6 months. Industry average: 71% (CIRR); top programs: 90–96% (Flatiron, General Assembly); this metric is the primary trust signal for employer partners and student decisions.

**Net Promoter Score (NPS):** Student satisfaction and referral likelihood (-100 to +100). EdTech SaaS median NPS 30–45; top bootcamps target 60+; K-12 private school target 50+. Below 20 signals churn risk and admissions pipeline decay.

**Accreditation Status:** Binary blocking node. Without regional accreditation, institution cannot offer Title IV-eligible federal financial aid. Timeline to initial accreditation: 2–7 years for HLC/SACSCOC/WASC; cost: $73K direct (AACSB) to $200K+ fully-loaded including compliance staff, systems, and consultant fees.

**Financial Responsibility Score (FRS):** U.S. Department of Education composite score (−1.0 to 3.0) measuring institutional financial health; score below 1.5 triggers enhanced cash monitoring for Title IV; score below 1.0 can result in provisional certification or loss of Title IV eligibility.

**Student-to-Instructor Ratio:** Online asynchronous: unlimited (recorded content); online cohort: target 20:1 to 30:1; in-person bootcamp: target 8:1 to 15:1; private K-12: avg 10:1; tutoring: 1:1. Ratio drives both cost structure and completion rate.

**Cohort Fill Rate:** Percent of available seats sold per cohort launch. Below 60% makes cohort financially marginal; break-even typically at 70–75% fill for a $15K tuition, 20-seat bootcamp with $113K/month operating cost. Trigger for cohort cancellation or marketing investment increase.

**Revenue Per Employee (RPE):** Coursera: ~$348K RPE (2024 $695M revenue, ~2,000 employees); lean online-only companies target $200K+; labor-intensive in-person schools often $80K–$120K RPE. Key investor metric post-Series A.

**Churn Rate (Subscription Products):** Monthly student/subscription churn. EdTech B2B SaaS: 9.6% annual average; consumer subscription: 15–25% annual; K-12 district contracts: 5–10% annual (budget cycle risk). LTV model breaks at >20% annual churn.

**State Authorization Count:** Number of U.S. states in which an institution is legally authorized to enroll students. Online programs must be authorized in each student's state. 50-state coverage requires $50K–$200K in legal fees; SARA (State Authorization Reciprocity Agreement) membership ($2,000–$15,000/yr) covers 49 states via a single application.

## Growth Stages

| Stage | Threshold | What Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| 0 — Solo Instructor | 1 person, 0–10 students, $0–$5K MRR | Home office; per-session billing; no employees; self-hosted or third-party platform (Teachable, Calendly) |
| 1 — Early Traction | 10–50 students, $5K–$30K MRR | First contract instructor; coworking space; basic LMS (Thinkific/Kajabi); social media marketing; first cohort model possible |
| 2 — Validated Model | 50–200 students OR $30K–$100K MRR | Dedicated classroom or online infrastructure; first full-time employee (admissions or ops); structured curriculum; monthly cadence; formal refund/cancellation policy |
| 3 — Growth Engine | 200–500 students OR $100K–$500K MRR | Marketing manager hired; CAC tracking begins; student success function; employer partnerships (bootcamp) or district pilots (K-12 SaaS); content production team |
| 4 — Institutional | 500–2,000 students OR $500K–$2M MRR | Accreditation application initiated (if degree/Title IV path); financial aid director; registrar; compliance officer; SIS deployment; Series A viable |
| 5 — Regional Authority | 2,000–10,000 students OR $2M–$8M MRR | Regional accreditation granted (if applicable); Title IV eligibility unlocked; Pell Grant revenue begins; OPM partnership possible; satellite campus or second city; employer brand established |
| 6 — Scaled Platform | 10,000–100,000 learners OR $8M–$50M MRR | Multi-product (degrees + certificates + corporate); full C-suite (CEO, CAO, CFO, CTO, CCO); enterprise LMS; data/analytics team; government grant pursuit; AACSB or specialized accreditation possible |
| 7 — Market Leader | 100,000+ learners OR $50M+ MRR | Coursera/2U/Udemy tier; international delivery; OPM relationships or owned online degree programs; IPO or PE-backed exit; content licensing to other institutions; testing center network |
