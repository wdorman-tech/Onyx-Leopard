## Industry-Specific Metrics

**Audience Size (Subscribers / Followers / Monthly Uniques)**
The foundational metric for all monetization. YouTube: monetization threshold 1,000 subs / 4,000 watch hours; brand deal viability begins ~10K–25K. Podcast: first ad interest at 3,000–5,000 downloads/episode; network quality threshold 10,000+. Newsletter: sponsorship interest at 5,000 subscribers; meaningful direct revenue at 25,000+. Web publishers: programmatic ad revenue viable at 100K+ monthly sessions; meaningful at 500K+.

**CPM (Cost Per Mille / Cost Per Thousand Impressions) — Advertiser-Side**
What advertisers pay. Display programmatic open auction: $1–$5. Display private marketplace: $5–$15. Pre-roll video: $10–$35. Connected TV: $25–$55. Podcast host-read 60-sec: $25–$50. Newsletter B2B niche: $40–$80. Host-read podcast 30-sec: $21–$24. Programmatic podcast DAI: $15–$25. Finance/investing content commands 2–4x general content CPMs.

**RPM (Revenue Per Mille) — Publisher-Side**
What publishers actually receive after platform cut. YouTube RPM: $2–$10 general content, $8–$25 finance/tech, $3–$7 gaming, $5–$15 lifestyle. Podcast RPM: $15–$40 depending on host-read vs. DAI and topic. Web display RPM: $2–$8. All RPMs are CPM minus platform take rate (YouTube takes 45%, Google takes ~32% of display, podcast networks take 25–30%).

**Engagement Rate**
Instagram: 1–3% healthy; <1% declining; 3–6% strong; micro-influencers (<100K) average 3–6%, mega (>1M) average 0.5–1.5%. YouTube: average view-through rate 30–50%; click-through rate on end screens 3–5%. Podcast: completion rate >60% is strong; 80%+ is exceptional. Newsletter: open rate 25–40% healthy for niche publishers; 15–25% for large consumer; click rate 2–5%.

**Subscriber Count / Download Count**
Podcast benchmark tiers: <1,000 downloads/ep (hobbyist), 1,000–3,000 (indie), 3,000–10,000 (growing), 10,000–50,000 (mid-tier, monetizable), 50,000–100,000 (premium inventory), 100,000+ (top 1% of all podcasts). YouTube: 100K is first milestone; 1M+ unlocks premium brand deals; 10M+ enables MrBeast-scale direct monetization. Newsletter: 10K = viable, 50K = attractive, 250K+ = acquisition target territory.

**Churn Rate (Subscription)**
Annual churn benchmarks for digital media: best-in-class news publishers 15–20% annual churn (NYT, The Atlantic). Average consumer publisher: 35% annual churn (Zuora data). Monthly churn >10% flagged as unsustainable by Digiday research (41% of publishers report this). Streaming platforms: Netflix ~2% monthly; Disney+ ~4–5% monthly. Podcast subscription: 5–8% monthly typical. Acceptable monthly churn for healthy subscription: <3% (36% annual).

**Content Velocity**
Output volume tracked by format. High-volume news publishers: 50–200 articles/day. Niche editorial brands: 5–20 articles/week. YouTube channels: 1–4 videos/week for growth phase. Podcasts: 1–2 episodes/week standard; daily for news. Newsletter: 3–5 emails/week for daily briefings; 1x/week for premium analysis. Production cost per minute of finished video: $300–$3,000/min for YouTube-quality; $3,000–$30,000/min for broadcast-quality scripted.

**Production Cost Per Minute**
Benchmark ranges by format: Podcast: $50–$300/finished minute (editing, mixing, show notes). YouTube vlog-style: $200–$500/min. YouTube premium (MrBeast-tier): $5,000–$50,000+/min. Branded video content: $1,500–$5,000/min (production + post). Scripted TV pilot: $20,000–$100,000+/min. Documentary: $3,000–$20,000/min. Animation: $5,000–$50,000+/min.

**IP Library Value**
Catalog-based media companies valued on content library. Rule of thumb: 2–5x annual licensing revenue for catalog value; streaming platforms pay 1.5–3x production cost for content with proven audiences. Warner Bros. Discovery, Paramount content write-downs in 2023–2024 demonstrated risks of overpaying for catalog. Music catalog valuations: 15–25x annual royalty income (historically). Podcast catalog: 3–8x annual ad revenue at peak (Spotify paid 100x revenue for The Ringer at acquisition in 2020).

**Ad Fill Rate**
Percentage of available ad impressions actually sold. Target: >85% for direct-sold + programmatic combined; open auction alone may fill 60–75%. Direct-sold fills at 100% against commitments but covers only 20–40% of inventory. Unfilled impressions = zero revenue. Header bidding implementation typically raises fill rate by 10–15%.

**CPL / CPA for Sponsorships**
Brand partners increasingly demand performance metrics beyond impressions. Podcast host-read ads: conversion rates 5–10x display; attributed CPA $30–$150 depending on vertical. Newsletter CPA: $15–$80 for B2B SaaS; higher for financial products. YouTube sponsorship: brand recall lift 10–30% (vs. 3–5% for display). Influencer marketing ROAS: 6.5–15x spend for product-adjacent creators.

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**
Subscription benchmarks: Patreon average ARPU ~$8–$12/mo. Substack average $10/mo ($96/yr). Streaming OTT owned platform $8–$20/mo. Top-tier newsletter subscriptions (professional/B2B): $30–$100/mo. The Athletic (acquired by NYT): ~$8/mo. Spotify Premium: ~$11/mo. Netflix standard: $15.49/mo. Ad-supported tier reduces ARPU 30–50% but widens addressable market.

The above is the full taxonomy for `/research/industries/media-content.md`. A few notes on the data:

**Where data conflicts**: The global podcast industry size shows two different numbers depending on what is being measured. IAB reports US podcast ad revenue at $2B+ (2024). Sounds Profitable reports $7.3B globally including subscription revenue, Patreon, live events, and all monetization attached to podcasters — not just advertising. Both are correct for their scope. Use IAB for ad-revenue-only modeling and $7B+ for total podcast creator economy.

**On gaming**: This taxonomy treats gaming studios as a sub-type within the media/content umbrella. If the simulation needs to model a dedicated gaming studio, the AA/AAA distinction creates a very different cost structure (25–600 people vs. 2–10) and a completely different revenue model (game sales + DLC + live service vs. advertising/subscriptions). A separate gaming-specific taxonomy would be warranted at that point.

**Most uncertain figures**: Live event production costs vary 10–100x based on scale and talent; sync licensing fees are the widest range in the dataset ($5 to $500,000+ for the same usage type); MCN revenue splits are highly negotiable and poorly disclosed publicly.
