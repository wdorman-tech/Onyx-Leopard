All financial triggers in the node tables above should be mapped to one or more of these metrics.\n\n**Rooms Performance**\n- **RevPAR** (Revenue Per Available Room): ADR × Occupancy. National avg $99.94 (2024). Economy $18–$35; midscale $50–$75; upscale $80–$120; luxury $210–$450+.\n- **ADR** (Average Daily Rate): Rooms revenue ÷ rooms sold. Economy $40–$80; midscale $75–$110; upscale $120–$175; full-service $175–$275; luxury $300–$600+; resort luxury $441 avg ADR (2024).\n- **Occupancy Rate**: Rooms sold ÷ rooms available. Industry avg 63% (2024); extended-stay avg 72–75%; luxury 70–75%.\n- **GOPPAR** (GOP Per Available Room): GOP ÷ available rooms. Primary investor return metric.\n- **TRevPAR** (Total Revenue Per Available Room): Total hotel revenue ÷ available rooms. Elevated by F&B, spa, parking, resort fees. Luxury ResortsPAR often 2–3x their RevPAR.\n- **Cost Per Occupied Room (CPOR)**: All rooms department costs ÷ occupied rooms. Benchmark: economy $25–$40; select-service $40–$60; full-service $60–$100; luxury $100–$180.\n- **RevPOR** (Revenue Per Occupied Room): Total revenue ÷ occupied rooms. Urban hotels $120–$180; luxury resorts $300+.\n\n**Profitability**\n- **GOP Margin**: Gross Operating Profit ÷ Total Revenue. Limited/select-service: 38–50% (or 26% per 2024 CBRE data for select-service); full-service: 25–35% (or 15% per CBRE 2024); luxury resort: 35–42%. Note: CBRE 2024 data shows margins declining as cost inflation outpaced revenue growth.\n- **EBITDA per Key**: Net operating income + D&A ÷ room count. Boutique independent median EBITDA: 26.6% of revenue; benchmark $8K–$35K/key/year depending on tier.\n- **Departmental Profit Margins**: Rooms dept 65–75%; F&B 27.5% (2024, declining); Spa 30–40%; Parking 70–80%.\n\n**Distribution**\n- **OTA vs. Direct Booking Ratio**: OTA slightly led in 2024 ($266B vs $262B gross bookings). Target for branded hotels: 40–60% direct. Independent target: 25–40% direct.\n- **Loyalty Contribution**: Branded hotels: 52.8% of occupied rooms from loyalty members (2024). Revenue contribution up to 60% of total for large chains.\n- **Channel Cost of Acquisition**: OTA 15–25% commission; GDS 10–12% + booking fee; direct web ~2–5% (booking engine + digital marketing cost); voice/direct 1–3%.\n\n**Operations**\n- **Rooms Per Employee Ratio**: Luxury 0.33–0.5 rooms/employee (2–3 employees/room); midscale 0.67–1.0; economy 1.25–3.33.\n- **Employee Turnover Rate**: Industry avg 70–80% annually; high turnover costs $1,500–$3,000/employee to replace.\n- **F&B Revenue Per Available Room**: Full-service benchmark $15–$50/available room/day depending on outlet mix.\n- **Review Score / NPS**: 1-star increase in ratings = 5–9% revenue increase (Harvard). Every 10% improvement in TripAdvisor score = 9–15% bookings increase.\n\n**Growth / Portfolio**\n- **Development Pipeline (Rooms)**: Q4 2024: 746,986 rooms in U.S. pipeline; record high.
