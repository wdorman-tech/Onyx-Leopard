> In real estate, "locations" are the core asset inventory — properties under development, managed assets, sales infrastructure, and geographic market presence. A property IS the product; the office is where you administer it.

## Industry-Specific Metrics

**Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)**
NOI divided by property value. Primary valuation metric for income-producing real estate. CBRE H1 2025 benchmarks: multifamily 5.0–5.5%, industrial 5.5–6.5%, suburban office 7.0–9.0%+, grocery-anchored retail 6.0–7.0%. Lower cap rate = higher valuation. A 50 bps cap rate compression on a $10M NOI asset = $10–$17M value increase.

**Net Operating Income (NOI)**
Gross revenue minus operating expenses (excluding debt service and depreciation). Target NOI margin 55–70% for stabilized multifamily. The single most important number in property valuation. NOI multiplied by exit cap rate = projected sale price.

**Gross Commission Income (GCI) Per Agent**
Total commissions generated by a single agent before brokerage split. Industry median $49,000/yr; top 10% agents: $250,000–$500,000+/yr GCI; $1M+ GCI agents are the top 1%. Brokerage profitability depends on GCI per agent exceeding desk cost (typically $1,500–$3,000/agent/month all-in).

**Transaction Volume**
Total dollar value of property sold or leased in a period. Brokerage ranking metric. Compass 2023: $200B+ in transaction volume. Top individual agent teams: $100M–$500M+ annually. National market recovery: transaction volume up 19% in 2025 (CBRE).

**Days on Market (DOM)**
Elapsed time from listing date to executed contract. Residential benchmark: 30–45 days healthy; under 14 days = seller's market; 90+ days = pricing problem or demand gap. Affects agent reputation, carrying costs, and seller satisfaction scores.

**Occupancy Rate**
Percentage of rentable units or square footage currently leased. Stabilized multifamily target: 93–96%. Industrial healthy: 95%+. Office: Class A major metro target 88–92%. Below 80% triggers lender covenant review. CBRE projects multifamily vacancy declining to 4.9% in 2025.

**Absorption Rate**
Speed at which available units or space is leased in a given market. Net absorption = new space occupied minus vacated space. Negative absorption = market contraction. High absorption allows rent growth; low absorption signals concession pressure.

**Lease-Up Velocity**
Leases signed per month during initial fill-up of new development. Target: 15–25 units/month for market-rate multifamily lease-up. Below target extends carry period and delays stabilization; lender covenants often require 90% occupancy within 12–18 months of CO.

**Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)**
NOI divided by annual debt service. Agency lenders (Fannie/Freddie) require minimum 1.25x DSCR. Below 1.0x = NOI does not cover debt (negative leverage). Construction loans underwrite to projected stabilized DSCR for takeout financing.

**Loan-to-Value (LTV) / Loan-to-Cost (LTC)**
LTV: loan balance divided by property value. LTC: construction loan divided by total project cost. Agency permanent loans: 65–80% LTV. Construction loans: 55–65% LTC. Higher LTV = more leverage but more risk at volatility. Developer equity requirement = 100% minus LTC.

**Rent Per Square Foot (PSF)**
Primary office, retail, and industrial performance benchmark. Gross rent PSF vs. effective rent PSF (net of concessions). Spread between asking and effective rent reveals true market softness. Multifamily equivalent: monthly rent per unit benchmarked against submarket avg.

**Equity Multiple**
Total cash returned to equity divided by total equity invested. Target for value-add: 1.5–2.0x over 5 years. Ground-up development target: 2.0–3.0x over 3–5 years. Below 1.5x unlevered is generally not worth development risk.

**Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**
Time-adjusted annualized return on equity. Institutional LP target: 15–20% levered IRR for value-add; 18–25%+ for ground-up development. Preferred return (hurdle) to LPs: typically 6–8% before promote kicks in.

**Revenue Per Unit (RPU)**
Total revenue (rent + ancillary) divided by unit count. NAA 2024 benchmark: ancillary income adds $1,482/unit/yr above base rent. Tracks NOI growth independent of occupancy fluctuation.

**Property Management Profit Margin**
PM revenue minus direct staffing, software, and overhead costs. Healthy PM company: 15–30% EBITDA margin. Scales favorably above 2,000 units — staff leverage improves; technology cost per unit declines.

**GCI Retention Rate (Brokerage)**
Percentage of GCI retained by brokerage after agent splits. High-split brokerages (eXp: 80% to agent): retain 20%. Traditional brokerages (50/50): retain 50% but must provide more infrastructure. Net contribution per agent = retained GCI minus agent-related overhead.

**Average Sales Price (ASP)**
Average transaction price in a market or brokerage. Directly drives GCI even at identical commission percentage. Luxury market agent: $3M ASP at 2.5% = $75,000 GCI/deal. Entry market agent: $300K ASP at same rate = $7,500 GCI/deal.

**Cost Per Lead (CPL) and Lead-to-Close Ratio**
Digital marketing efficiency metric. CPL: $10–$200/lead depending on source (Zillow, Google Ads, organic). Lead-to-close: top agents 1–5%; typical agents 0.5–1%. Drives marketing budget allocation decisions.
