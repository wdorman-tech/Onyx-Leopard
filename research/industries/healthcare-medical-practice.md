## Locations

| Type | Trigger | Monthly Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solo Physician Office | Stage 1 (Founding) | $8,000–$12,000 | Baseline capacity 15–20 patients/day; requires 1 MD + minimal staff |
| Single-Specialty Clinic | 2+ physicians OR panel > 800 | $15,000–$25,000 | Capacity 30–50/day; unlocks subspecialty billing codes |
| Multi-Specialty Group Practice | 5+ physicians, 3+ specialties, OR revenue > $2M/yr | $30,000–$60,000 | Cross-referral revenue; risk pool for VBC contracts |
| Urgent Care Center | Market research + $350K–$800K capital | $35,000–$50,000/site | Walk-in 40–80 patients/day; 25-month breakeven typical |
| FQHC (Federally Qualified Health Center) | 340B enrollment + HRSA designation | $20,000–$40,000 (offset by grants) | 340B drug pricing discount 20–50%; Medicaid cost-based reimbursement |
| Ambulatory Surgery Center (ASC) | AAAHC/JCAHO accreditation + CMS cert + 2+ surgical specialties | $80,000–$150,000 | High-margin procedural revenue (ASC reimburse ~60% of hospital outpatient) |
| Diagnostic Imaging Center | 2+ referring physicians + MRI/CT capital ($2.5–3M) | $250,000–$370,000 | Imaging revenue; Stark Law in-office exception compliance required |
| In-House Clinical Lab | CLIA certificate + lab director | $8,000–$20,000 | Reduces reference lab costs 40–60%; generates ancillary lab revenue |
| Telehealth Platform | Telehealth licensing per state + EHR telehealth module | $3,000–$8,000 | +20–40% patient touchpoints without facility cost; CPT 99213/99214 equivalent |
| Satellite Clinic / Spoke Site | Hub clinic > 12 months + 1 provider assigned | $10,000–$18,000 | Extends geographic reach; feeds referrals to hub |
| Concierge / DPC Practice | 200+ patients willing to pay membership | $5,000–$15,000 | Direct membership $70–$200/patient/mo; eliminates billing overhead |
| Occupational Health Clinic | Employer contract + DOT physician certification | $12,000–$20,000 | B2B revenue from employer contracts; workers' comp billing |
| Behavioral Health Satellite | Psychiatrist/LCSW credentialed + BH billing codes | $8,000–$18,000 | Mental health parity billing; collaborative care model |
| Infusion Center | Oncologist/rheumatologist + specialty pharmacy contract | $40,000–$80,000 | High-margin drug administration revenue (J-codes) |
| Mobile Clinic / Van | Vehicle ($150K–$200K) + per-jurisdiction licensing | $8,000–$15,000 | Reaches underserved populations; Medicaid outreach billing |

## External Partners

### Credentialing & Licensing (Hard Blockers)

| Type | Trigger | Monthly Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| State Medical License | Founding (per physician, per state) | $500–$1,000 (one-time, renewal ~$500/2yr) | Cannot practice without; required before payer enrollment |
| DEA Registration | Any controlled substance prescribing | ~$25/mo amortized | Required for Schedule II–V prescriptions |
| NPI (Type 1 + Type 2) | Founding | Free | Hard prerequisite for all payer billing |
| CLIA Certificate | In-house lab launch | $12–$150/mo | Legally required for any lab testing |

### Payer Enrollment

| Type | Trigger | Monthly Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Medicare Enrollment (PECOS) | NPI active; physician licensed | $0; 30–90 days | Unlocks all Medicare billing; re-verify every 5 years |
| Medicaid Enrollment | PECOS complete OR state application | $0; 60–120 days per state | Unlocks Medicaid billing; varies dramatically by state |
| Commercial Payer Credentialing (1st) | NPI + PECOS + malpractice active | $200–$500/provider; 90–120 days | Unlocks largest commercial revenue stream; hardest operational blocker |
| Commercial Payer (Additional) | 1st commercial complete | $150–$400/provider/payer | Each expands billable base; 3–5 payers covers 80% of commercially insured |
| MCO Contract | Medicaid enrolled + panel in MCO area | Negotiated PMPM | Access to Medicaid managed care; capitation possible |

## Industry-Specific Metrics

- **Days in AR**: (Total AR / Avg Daily Charges). Healthy <30 days; typical 35–40; warning >50.
- **Net Collection Rate**: (Net Collections / Net Charges). Healthy >95%; warning <90%.
- **Denial Rate**: (Denied Claims / Total Submitted). Best practice <5%; critical >10%.
- **First-Pass Resolution Rate**: (Claims Paid First Submission / Total). Target >90%.
- **wRVU Production**: Primary care 4,000–5,500/yr; underperforming <3,500.
- **Revenue per wRVU**: Primary care $45–$65; low <$40 signals poor payer mix.
- **Payer Mix**: Commercial >50% healthy; Medicaid >40% without FQHC = margin risk.
- **Patient Volume**: Primary care 18–22/day; <12 underutilized; >28 burnout risk.
- **No-Show Rate**: Healthy <8%; warning >15%. Each no-show = ~$150 lost.
- **Panel Size**: Standard FFS 1,500–2,500; DPC 300–600.
- **Overhead Percentage**: Multi-specialty 55–65%; >70% = depleting capital.
- **Staff-to-Provider Ratio**: Standard 4.5–5.5:1; >7:1 overstaffed; <3:1 understaffed.
- **MIPS Composite Score**: >75 = positive adjustment; <18.75 = maximum -9% penalty.
- **Credentialing Status**: 100% target; <70% = revenue gap risk.

## Growth Stages

| Stage | Threshold | What Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| 0 — Pre-Practice | 0 patients; licenses pending | NPI; state license; malpractice; EHR selection; PECOS application |
| 1 — Solo Practice | 1 physician; PECOS + 1 commercial payer; <800 panel | Medicare/Medicaid billing; MA; front desk; basic lab (CLIA waiver) |
| 2 — Small Group | 2–5 physicians; 1,500–4,000 panel; 3+ payers; $500K–$2M/yr | NP/PA; RN; in-house biller; credentialing coordinator; CCM program; PCMH |
| 3 — Established Multi-Provider | 5–15 physicians; 2+ locations; $2M–$8M/yr | Compliance officer; HR; IT; Director of Quality; ACO application; imaging planning |
| 4 — Regional Group | 15–50 physicians; 3–10 locations; $8M–$30M/yr | CEO; CFO; COO; ASC; imaging center; specialty pharmacy; VBC contracts; Epic migration |
| 5 — Multi-Specialty System | 50+ physicians; 10+ locations; >$30M/yr | CIO/CISO; GME affiliation; full VBC risk (capitation); direct-to-employer; 340B |
| 6 — Integrated Delivery Network | Hospital affiliation; 100K+ covered lives; >$100M/yr | ACO full risk; Medicare Advantage partnership; AI population health; national brand |
