## Industry-Specific Metrics

- **Operating Ratio (OR)**: Total operating expenses divided by total revenue. Lower is better. LTL benchmark: ODFL and Saia target below 80–85%; dry van truckload average was loss-making in 2024 (negative margin per ATRI); LTL leaders: XPO at 86.2% OR (Q4 2024), Saia at 85.0% for full year 2024. An OR above 97–98% signals near-insolvency.

- **Revenue Per Truck Per Week**: Standard carrier productivity metric. Owner-operators averaged $5,000–$7,000 gross/week; ATRI 2024 data pegs total operating cost at $2.26/mile all-in; revenue must exceed this figure for profitability. Expedited carriers track this per tractor and saw 5% declines in 2024 due to utilization drops.

- **Empty Miles / Deadhead Percentage**: Empty miles as a percentage of total miles driven. Industry average rose to 16.7% in 2024 (ATRI). Small fleets under 5 trucks achieve lowest deadhead at 10%; larger OTR networks average 15–25%; every deadhead mile costs $0.50–$1.00 in unrecovered cost.

- **Driver Turnover Rate**: Annual percentage of drivers who leave. Large carrier (100+ trucks) average was 48% in 2024 (up from 46.6% in 2023); OTR long-haul carriers historically near 94% annually; regional and dedicated operations significantly lower (20–35%); replacing one driver costs $8,000–$15,000 in recruiting, onboarding, and training.

- **Freight Brokerage Gross Margin**: Shipper rate minus carrier pay divided by shipper rate. Industry average compressed to 13–15% in 2024 (DAT analytics); 13.0% on dry van, 15.1% on flatbed, 13.8% on reefer (2023 DAT data); break-even gross margin per load approximately $211. Peak cycle margins reached 18–22% in 2021.

- **Cost Per Mile (Total)**: All-in operating cost per revenue mile. ATRI 2024: $2.260/mile total; breakdown — driver wages 79.6¢, fuel 48¢, equipment payments 39¢, repair/maintenance ~20¢, driver benefits ~20¢, insurance ~9¢, tires ~6¢, permits/licenses ~3¢.

- **Fleet Utilization Rate**: Percentage of trucks/trailers generating revenue on any given day. Target 85–95% for asset-based carriers; drops below 70% during freight recessions; trailer utilization often tracked separately (drop trailer programs aim for 90%+).

- **Claims Ratio**: Total cargo claims paid divided by carrier revenue. Industry metric tracked by Synchrogistics LTL Claims Index (first public LTL claims benchmark); cargo theft surged 27% in 2024; average LSP cargo theft loss ~$2M/year; insurance premiums up 12.5% year-over-year in 2024; per-mile insurance premiums up ~50% over the past decade.

- **Load-to-Truck Ratio (DAT)**: Available loads divided by available trucks on DAT load board at a given time. Ratios above 3:1 signal tight capacity (carrier's market); ratios below 2:1 signal loose capacity (shipper's market); 2024 remained in loose market (1.5–2.5:1 range), suppressing spot rates below contract.

- **Days to Pay / Cash Conversion Cycle**: Average time from load delivery to cash collection. Industry standard broker/shipper payment terms 30–45 days; factoring converts to same-day or next-day; unfactored owner-operators face severe cash flow compression at 30–60 day payment cycles.

- **CSA Score (Compliance, Safety, Accountability)**: FMCSA's percentile ranking of carrier safety performance across 7 BASIC categories (unsafe driving, HOS compliance, vehicle maintenance, driver fitness, controlled substances, hazmat, crash indicator). Scores above 65th percentile trigger interventions; poor scores increase insurance premiums 20–50% and cause shippers to avoid the carrier.

- **Revenue Per Load / Gross Profit Per Load**: Brokerage: gross margin per load; average break-even ~$211/load; top brokerages target $300–$500+/load. Asset carriers: revenue per loaded mile target $2.50–$3.50 in current market; spot market dry van ~$1.92/mile (2025).

## Growth Stages

| Stage | Threshold | What Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1: Owner-Operator / Solo Broker | 1 truck or 1 broker agent; <$500K annual revenue | FMCSA authority, basic ELD ($20–$50/truck), DAT/Truckstop subscription, factoring relationship; home office viable; spot market only; owner drives or books loads personally |
| Stage 2: Micro-Fleet / Boutique Brokerage | 2–5 trucks OR 2–5 broker agents; $500K–$2M revenue | First dedicated dispatcher or ops hire; shared office; company driver hire; basic TMS (SaaS, $69–$500/month); IRP plates; UCR filing; first carrier relationships if brokerage |
| Stage 3: Small Fleet / Small Brokerage | 5–15 trucks OR $2M–$5M brokerage revenue | Safety/compliance role emerges (part-time or outsourced DOT consulting); dedicated office lease; drug testing consortium; first controller-level accounting; fuel card program; ELD fleet management; carrier monitoring (Carrier411); first contract lane relationships |
| Stage 4: Growing Fleet / Regional Brokerage | 15–40 trucks OR $5M–$15M revenue | Full-time safety director; driver manager role; in-house maintenance begins (10–15 trucks justify shop lease); HR/recruiting coordinator; structured sales team; TMS with EDI; customer service department; drop trailer program possible; freight factoring may be retired for bank line of credit |
| Stage 5: Regional Carrier / Mid-Size Brokerage | 40–100 trucks OR $15M–$50M revenue | Terminal/cross-dock facility; regional hub locations; fleet maintenance shop (owned bays); controller + VP Operations; dedicated pricing analyst; formal claims management; intermodal partnerships possible; own broker arm if asset carrier; OR tracking by division; carrier scorecard programs with shippers |
| Stage 6: Multi-Regional Carrier / Large Brokerage | 100–500 trucks OR $50M–$250M revenue | Multiple terminal locations; CFO hire; formal safety department (team); centralized dispatch with shift supervisors; dedicated IT/TMS team; fleet financing desk; warehousing/3PL services; dedicated fleet (DCC) offerings; LTL network possible; intermodal assets or rail agreements; company-sponsored CDL training program |
| Stage 7: Large Carrier / Super-Broker | 500–2,000 trucks OR $250M–$1B revenue | National hub-and-spoke network; owned fuel islands; in-house maintenance fleet; advanced analytics/pricing team; formal HR with talent acquisition function; supply chain solutions (managed transportation); freight forwarding arm; own factoring/financial services product; autonomous/technology investment |
| Stage 8: Enterprise / Publicly Traded | 2,000+ trucks OR $1B+ revenue (Werner, XPO, J.B. Hunt territory) | J.B. Hunt: 6,153 company tractors, 122,272 trailing equipment units, $12B TTM revenue (2025); multiple business units (JBI intermodal, DCS dedicated, ICS brokerage, FMS fleet management); technology platform as product; board governance; debt capital markets; M&A as growth lever; public safety reporting; ESG disclosure |
