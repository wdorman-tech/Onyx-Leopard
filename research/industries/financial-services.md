## Revenue Streams

| Type | Trigger | Monthly Cost (Net) | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AUM Management Fee (0.50%–1.25% annually)** | First paying client | Positive from day 1 | Core revenue; 92% of RIAs use AUM fees; 86% rely on it as primary source (IAA 2024); tiered: 1.0–1.25% on first $1M, declining to 0.50–0.75% above $5M; average 1.02–1.05% across portfolios |
| **Financial Planning Fee (Flat Annual / Retainer)** | Comprehensive planning capability established | $1,500–$8,000/client/year | 45% of SEC-registered RIAs now charge fixed fees (up 9 pts since 2000); growing for mass-affluent and next-gen clients who lack investable assets; decouples revenue from market performance |
| **Subscription / Monthly Retainer Fee** | Younger client segment OR XY Planning Network model | $100–$500/client/month | Recurring revenue not tied to AUM; growing among advisors targeting millennials and early-career professionals; $1,200–$6,000/client annually |
| **Hourly Planning Fee** | Ad hoc planning engagements or one-time consultations | $200–$500/hour | Used by 30–40% of RIAs as supplement; less predictable; common for project-based work (divorce, inheritance, retirement transition) |
| **Insurance Commission (First-Year)** | Life, disability, or long-term care insurance placed | 55–120% of first-year premium (life); 3–7% (health) | One-time revenue spike; creates ongoing renewal commissions (5–20%); requires insurance license; compliance consideration for fee-only RIAs |
| **Insurance Renewal Commission** | Ongoing client policies in force | 5–20% of annual premium | Recurring passive revenue; builds over time as insurance book grows; may conflict with fee-only fiduciary positioning |
| **Performance Fee / Fulcrum Fee** | Qualified client (>$2.2M net worth); hedge fund or separately managed account | Positive above hurdle rate | 20% of gains above 8% hurdle is standard; only available to accredited investors under Dodd-Frank; SEC limits who can be charged performance fees |
| **Carried Interest (Private Fund)** | Private equity or hedge fund vehicle launched | 20% of profits above 8% hurdle | Standard industry: 84% of PE funds use 8% preferred return (Callan 2024); only earned on exit/distribution; long-dated revenue (5–10 year fund cycles) |
| **Management Fee (Private Fund)** | Private fund launch | 1.5–2.0% of committed capital/year | Mean 1.74% for buyout, 1.93% for growth equity (Callan 2024); covers fund operating costs during investment period; shifts to NAV-based after investment period |
| **Trading Commission / Transaction Fee** | Hybrid RIA with BD affiliation OR legacy commission business | $0 (zero-commission era) to $25–$50/ticket for complex instruments | Near-zero for standard equities (Schwab/Fidelity); annuity and alternative product commissions still significant; regulatory pressure toward fee-only models |
| **Referral Fee (Solicitor Arrangement)** | Formal solicitor agreement under Advisers Act Rule 206(4)-3 | 20–50% of advisory fee from referred clients | Paid to CPAs, attorneys, other advisors; requires disclosure and written agreement; creates paid COI network |
| **OCIO / Institutional Management Fee** | Pension, endowment, foundation, or family office mandate | 0.21–0.50% AUM/year for $50M–$100M mandates (Cerulli) | Lower fee rate but large mandate size; U.S. OCIO assets projected at $3T+ (Cerulli 2024); winning an institutional mandate transforms firm economics |
| **Subscription Platform / SaaS Fee (Fintech Lender)** | Platform lender model | $500–$5,000/month + origination % | Charges for loan origination platform access or per-loan fees; fintech lender efficiency gains 70% faster processing, 30% lower costs vs traditional |
| **Loan Origination Fee (Fintech Lender)** | Consumer or SMB lending vertical | 1–5% of loan amount | One-time at closing; primary revenue for marketplace lenders; varies by credit quality, loan size, product type |
| **Interest Spread / Net Interest Income (Fintech Lender)** | Lending balance sheet established | Positive if cost of capital < yield | Core fintech lender revenue; difference between borrowing rate and lending rate; requires balance sheet or warehouse facility |
| **Wrap Fee Program** | Comprehensive investment management + planning bundle | 1.0–2.0% AUM all-in | Bundles custody, trading, advice, and planning into one fee; Raymond James, UBS, and independent RIAs use; simplifies billing; limits transparency |
| **Financial Planning Software / Portal Licensing (B2B SaaS)** | RIA with proprietary technology capability | $50–$200/client/month | RIAs that build proprietary client portals or planning tools and license to smaller advisors; rare but growing as aggregators scale |

## Industry-Specific Metrics

- **AUM per Advisor**: The benchmark for a productive senior advisor is $100M–$200M in managed AUM. Supported advisors (with paraplanner + CSA) frequently reach $150M–$200M; unsupported solo advisors plateau near $80M–$100M. At $500M+ AUM per advisor, the advisor is operating at top-decile efficiency and likely managing UHNW/family office relationships.

- **Revenue per Advisor**: Kitces Research 2024 shows median revenue per advisor of $400,000/year. Unsupported solos average $182,500. The "1+2 model" (one lead advisor, two support staff) generates over $1.2M annually. The 80th percentile financial planner earns $152,000 base salary plus $216,000 total compensation (Schwab 2024).

- **Revenue per Employee**: Jumped 24% in recent years from $250,000 to $310,000 across RIA firms (Kitces 2024). Well-run firms target $300,000–$500,000 revenue per employee. Below $200,000/employee signals staffing inefficiency or growth investment phase.

- **AUM Fee Rate (Blended)**: Industry average blended fee is 1.02–1.05% of AUM. 62% of advisors charge 1%+ on $1M portfolios, falling to 32% on $2M portfolios. Fee compression is real: 83% of advisors expect to charge under 1% on $5M+ portfolios by 2026 (Cerulli). Average fee for $10M+ portfolios trending toward 0.66%.

- **Operating Margin**: Healthy RIA targets 20–30% operating margin. Fidelity 2024 reported advisory expenses reached 82% of revenue at smaller RIAs (<$1B AUM), leaving only 18% margin — a record low. Larger, more efficient firms ($1B+) maintain 25–35% margins through scale.

- **Client Retention Rate**: Industry benchmark is 97% annual client retention (Schwab 2024). Top-performing firms hit 98–99%. Below 95% is a red flag indicating service or fee dissatisfaction.

- **Client Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: Industry average $743/client (Kitces research). Referral-acquired clients average $609 in marketing spend. Growth-focused firms invest $997+/client. Traditional marketing (seminars, direct mail) can exceed $2,000/client. Targeted digital lead generation can be as low as $250/client. Advisors with a defined marketing strategy added 50% more clients than peers without one.

- **Marketing Budget as % of Revenue**: Typical range 1–10% of annual revenue. Average solo advisor spends $9,000/year; advisory teams spend $23,200/year; industry average $15,908/year (Kitces 2024).

- **Organic Growth Rate**: Firms <$250M AUM grew 9.2% organically in 2024; firms >$250M grew 5% organically; top performers grew 12.5%. Market appreciation contributed additional AUM growth of 16.6% in 2024 (Schwab).

- **Compensation as % of Revenue**: Historically ~71% of total revenue across all firm sizes (IAA). Larger firms with leverage ratios (senior advisor + juniors + support) lower this to 55–65%. Owner-operators who count distributions as compensation skew this metric higher.

- **Assets per Client**: Average assets per client declining at smaller RIAs, where sub-$250K clients consume disproportionate service resources. HNW-focused firms ($1M+ minimum) achieve the highest revenue-per-client ratios. 80%+ of managed assets owned by clients aged 50+ (Fidelity 2024).

- **RIA Valuation Multiple**: Median EBITDA multiple reached 11.0x in 2024 (up from 9.9x in 2023). Revenue multiples range 2x–6x gross revenue; EBITDA multiples 8x–15x depending on firm size. Firms with $3B–$20B AUM can achieve high-teens EBITDA multiples. Recurring revenue base drives premium valuations: hybrid firms (commission + fee) valued 20% below pure fee-only equivalents. RIA M&A hit 366 deals in 2024, a record; Q1 2025 alone saw 118 transactions.

- **Compliance Cost per Employee**: Outsourced CCO retainer: $1,500–$6,000/month. In-house CCO median total comp: $122,000 (COMPLY data); can reach $226,000+ at large firms. Compliance software: $195–$500/month base (COMPLY RIA). Total compliance budget for a mid-size RIA: $150,000–$400,000/year including personnel, software, legal, and audit. 50% of advisory firms expect new SEC rules to push annual compliance costs to $100,000+.

- **Technology Spend as % of Revenue**: Financial services firms allocate 10–15% of total budget to IT and cybersecurity (IANS Research 2025). 96% of firms allocate more than 5% to IT/cyber. Wealth management industry expected to spend $24B+ on technology annually. Cybersecurity budgets grew 8% in 2024.

- **Fee Schedule Compression Velocity**: The percentage of AUM-fee-only firms declining; 45.2% of SEC-registered RIAs now charge fixed fees (IAA, 2024). Over 85% of planning-focused RIAs offer at least one non-AUM fee option. Monthly subscription fee adoption growing fastest among advisors targeting clients aged 25–45.

- **Leverage Ratio (Advisors to Support Staff)**: Best practice is 1 lead advisor supported by 2 staff (Kitces "1+2 model"). Top-quartile firms achieve revenue of $1.2M+ with this model. Solo unsupported advisors generate roughly 1/3 of this revenue potential.

## Growth Stages

| Stage | Threshold | What Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| **0 — Pre-Launch / Breakaway** | 0 AUM; advisor leaving wirehouse or BD | Home office or virtual address; IARD registration filing (state or SEC); E&O insurance purchase; custodian agreement; CRM setup; financial planning software |
| **1 — Solo Startup** | $0–$25M AUM; 1 advisor, 0–1 staff | State RIA registration (typically); Redtail or Wealthbox CRM; RightCapital or MoneyGuidePro; custodian at Schwab or Altruist; compliance software (COMPLY $195/month); E&O + cyber insurance ($320/month combined); revenue capped ~$182,500 unsupported |
| **2 — Emerging Solo** | $25M–$100M AUM; 1 advisor + 1 paraplanner/CSA | Dedicated office space ($1,500–$3,000/month); SEC registration likely not yet required; paraplanner hire unlocks 50+ client capacity; revenue approaching $250,000–$350,000; first formal compliance manual |
| **3 — Ensemble Practice** | $100M–$300M AUM; 3–8 employees | Dedicated office lease; first full-time CSA and operations staff; portfolio management software (Orion/Tamarac); associate advisor hire; outsourced CCO relationship; compliance costs $50,000–$100,000/year; AUM fee revenue $750,000–$2M/year |
| **4 — SEC-Registered RIA** | $110M AUM threshold (mandatory SEC registration) | SEC Form ADV Part 1, 2A, 2B; IARD filing; annual amendment; SEC examination risk increases; compliance program must meet investment adviser standards; D&O insurance recommended |
| **5 — Growing Multi-Advisor Firm** | $300M–$750M AUM; 10–20 employees | Regional office or flagship space ($8,000–$20,000/month); in-house CCO hire ($122,000–$226,000/year); CIO or OCIO relationship; Bloomberg or FactSet terminal; dedicated marketing function; formal client segmentation (A/B/C tier); first performance fee clients (if any) |
| **6 — $1B AUM Milestone** | $1B AUM; 20–35 employees | Full C-suite buildout (CCO, COO, CFO); institutional sales capability; private fund option viable; director of operations; formal HR function; branch office consideration; revenue $7M–$12M/year; M&A target attractiveness spikes; EBITDA multiple 10–12x |
| **7 — Regional Powerhouse** | $1B–$5B AUM; 35–75 employees | Multiple branch offices; trading desk; in-house CIO team; direct indexing capability; institutional/OCIO business line; alternative investment platform; aggregator partnership or PE investment likely; family office services launched |
| **8 — National Platform** | $5B–$25B AUM; 100+ employees | Corporate headquarters; national brand; institutional consultant coverage; SEC focus on examination; sophisticated compliance department with 3–5 staff; full alternative investment platform; M&A acquisition capability; valuation $50M–$250M+ |
| **9 — Enterprise / Aggregator** | $25B+ AUM or aggregator model (Focus Financial, Mercer Advisors, Creative Planning scale) | Consolidator M&A engine (Mercer: 100+ acquisitions since 2016, $70B AUM, 100+ locations); shared services infrastructure; PE-backed capital; RIA consolidators hold $1.5T AUM total (Cerulli 2024); international offices; proprietary technology platform; OCIO institutional business |
