## Industry-Specific Metrics

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
Average DTC CAC ranges $45–$90 across verticals. By category: pet $23, beauty $42, food/beverage $51, supplements $89. Meta median CPA in 2024 was $38.19 (Triple Whale, 30K brand dataset). CAC has risen ~40% over the past two years. Healthy brands target CAC < 33% of first-order AOV.

**LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)**
Healthy DTC brands target LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1. Ratios of 2:1–4:1 are considered acceptable depending on payback period. Subscription brands typically achieve LTV 3–5x that of one-time purchasers. 12-month LTV is the most common tracking window; 24-month for subscription.

**LTV:CAC Ratio**
Benchmark target: 3:1 minimum. Sub-2:1 signals unprofitable acquisition at scale. 4:1+ suggests room to increase spend. Seed-stage investors evaluate this ratio as a primary signal of business model viability.

**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**
Meta average ROAS: 2–5x (platform-reported); true ROAS closer to 1.5–3.5x post-attribution correction. Google Shopping: 4–8x. TikTok short-term: 4–12x, long-term: 3–5x. Blended ROAS target for profitable growth: 2.5–4x depending on contribution margin.

**Blended / True ROAS**
Platform-reported ROAS overstates true performance by 20–50% due to attribution overlap. Triple Whale / Northbeam correction is required above $30K/mo in spend. True ROAS = Total Revenue / Total Ad Spend (all channels combined).

**AOV (Average Order Value)**
DTC global median AOV ~$74–$150 depending on category (Triple Whale brands median $74.12 in 2024). Health & Beauty AOV $164. Home & Garden $146. Higher AOV brands can sustain higher CAC. Bundles and subscriptions are primary AOV-lifting levers. Target: AOV > 3x shipping cost.

**Conversion Rate**
Average Shopify store: 1.4–1.8%. Top-quartile stores: 3–5%. By channel: email traffic 5–7%, organic search 3–5%, paid social 0.8–1.5%, paid search 2–4%, direct 3–5%. Conversion rate is the highest-leverage single metric for CAC reduction.

**Gross Margin**
DTC category median gross margins: apparel 45–60%, beauty/skincare 65–75%, supplements/consumables 55–70%, furniture/home 35–50%, footwear 40–55%. 8-figure brand median: 56% gross margin. 7-figure brand median: 52%.

**Contribution Margin (CM2 / CM3)**
Contribution Margin 2 (post-variable costs including shipping, returns, payment processing): target 25–40% for healthy DTC. Contribution Margin 3 (post all variable + fixed marketing): target 15–25% at scale. Health & wellness brands average ~47.7% CM2. Mid-market average 28% in 2024.

**Ad Spend as % of Revenue**
DTC brands typically spend 15–30% of revenue on paid media at growth stage. Efficient at-scale brands target 10–20%. Ad spend >35% of revenue is a red flag for unit economics. B2C companies overall: 5–12% of revenue on all marketing.

**Return Rate**
US ecommerce average return rate: 16.9–20.4% of orders in 2024. Apparel returns: 30–40%. Consumables / beauty: 5–10%. Each return costs $20–30 to process (shipping + labor + restock). Returns > 25% of orders trigger margin erosion requiring pricing or policy intervention.

**Churn Rate (Subscription)**
DTC subscription monthly churn: 5–10%. Target: below 5%. Annual churn 40–60% is common; best-in-class brands achieve 20–30% annual churn. Consumer subscription churn is structurally higher than B2B SaaS (5–10% vs 2–4%).

**Repeat Purchase Rate / Retention**
DTC average repeat purchase rate: 28–32%. Top performers: 35%+. Email and SMS are the primary retention levers. A 5% improvement in retention rate can increase LTV by 25–95% (compound effect over 24 months).

**Payback Period (CAC Payback)**
Target: recover CAC within first 6–12 months for non-subscription; within 3–6 months for subscription. Payback > 18 months without a subscription model signals growth capital inefficiency.

**Inventory Turnover**
Healthy DTC: 6–12x annually. Low turnover (<4x) signals overstock and cash drag. High turnover (>15x) risks stockouts and lost revenue. Supplements and consumables turn faster than apparel or home goods.

**Email Revenue as % of Total**
Best-in-class DTC brands: email generates 25–40% of total revenue. Average: 15–25%. Brands below 15% are over-indexed on paid acquisition. Klaviyo is the dominant platform (used by >150K merchants).

## Growth Stages

| Stage | Threshold | What Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| 0 — Pre-Launch / Side Hustle | $0 revenue; 1 founder | Shopify Basic store; home office; first supplier relationship; social media accounts; brand identity |
| 1 — Launch & Proof | $0–$10K/mo revenue; 1–3 employees; 50–200 orders/mo | First paid Meta ad; Klaviyo email; manual fulfillment from home/garage; product-market fit testing |
| 2 — Early Traction | $10K–$50K/mo revenue; 3–6 employees; 200–1,000 orders/mo | First 3PL contract; performance marketing hire; UGC/influencer gifting; email list to 5K+; basic attribution setup |
| 3 — Growth Engine | $50K–$200K/mo revenue; 6–15 employees; 1,000–4,000 orders/mo | Multi-channel paid (Meta + Google + TikTok); Klaviyo flows fully built; CX hire; subscription product launch; first pop-up; Shopify Advanced |
| 4 — Scaling | $200K–$500K/mo revenue; 15–30 employees; 4,000–10,000 orders/mo | Head of Growth; Creative Director; bi-coastal 3PL; Triple Whale attribution; affiliate program; wholesale inquiry inbound; Series A-equivalent capital event |
| 5 — Mid-Market DTC | $500K–$1.5M/mo revenue; 30–60 employees; 10,000–25,000 orders/mo | Shopify Plus; owned fulfillment center evaluation; wholesale channel formalized; Amazon FBA launched; PR agency; Northbeam attribution; international shipping piloted |
| 6 — Omnichannel Brand | $1.5M–$5M/mo revenue; 60–150 employees; 25,000–75,000 orders/mo | Flagship retail store(s); VP Marketing + VP Ops + CFO; owned fulfillment center operational; Amazon DSP; EU warehouse; loyalty program; CMO hire; potential Series B or PE interest |
| 7 — Scaled Platform | $5M+/mo revenue; 150+ employees; 75,000+ orders/mo | Multiple retail locations (Warby Parker model: 300 stores); international DTC storefronts; licensing agreements; M&A target or IPO preparation; board of directors formalized; mass retail (Target/Walmart) partnership |
